CODES Framework

BombParty's incident prioritization system. When an incident is reported, the first question is always: what CODES category does this fall into? The category determines response speed, escalation path, and communication cadence.

🔤

The Five Categories

Classify every incident the moment it is reported
O

Orders  SEV 1 default

Reps or customers cannot place orders. Revenue is actively at risk. HIGHEST PRIORITY.

C

Code  SEV 1–2

Core business logic errors affecting multiple users or tenants simultaneously.

D

Data  SEV 1–2

Data sync failures, corruption, or data loss between systems.

E

Enrollment  SEV 1–2

Reps or customers cannot sign up or activate. Growth at risk.

S

Strategic  Immediate

Board or executive directive bypassing normal escalation. Immediate regardless of time or day.

🔍

How to Use CODES — Step by Step

Follow this every time an incident is reported
1
Incident is reported
By a rep, monitoring system, engineering, or you detect it personally.
2
Classify the CODES category
Is revenue at risk (O)? Is data corrupted (D)? Is enrollment broken (E)? Is it a core logic error (C)? Did Alan or the board direct this (S)?
3
Determine severity
See the Severity Levels & SLAs tab. CODES-O = almost always SEV 1.
4
Create the Jira ticket in APPSUP
Set the CODES label (CODES-O, CODES-C, etc.) + the SEV label (SEV1, SEV2, SEV3) + the correct component.
5
Execute the SOP for that severity level
The SOP tells you exactly who to call, when, and what to say. See the Incident SOPs tab.
